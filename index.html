What is Tremer?

A pocket-sized motion lab for people with Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's Disease (PD) is a progressive neurological condition that gradually changes the way a person moves, speaks and expresses themselves. Today, those changes are usually measured only during short clinic visits — a tiny window into a 24-hour life. Tremer closes that gap. By turning any smartphone into a clinically useful recording tool, Tremer lets patients capture how they actually feel and move at home, every day. A computer-vision library identifies the user's motion, and an AI model converts the recording into structured, comparable results that both patients and clinicians can act on.

Core features

Designed for daily, at-home use

Every feature in Tremer is built around one goal: making it effortless for patients to record, and meaningful for clinicians to review.

📊

Personalised dashboard

At a glance: condition status, total sessions logged, alerts that need attention, and reminders to record today. The patient always knows where they stand.

📦

Inside the app

A walk through every key screen

Real screens, captured from the live app.

Screen 01 · Home dashboard

Your health, summarised in one view

The home screen is the patient's daily anchor. It opens with the Tremer hero, then surfaces the most important information in four glanceable cards: current condition, total sessions logged, open alerts, and the daily record reminder.

  • Recent activity feed shows the last few completed sessions, doctor flags and results.
  • "Your Health Scores" card lets the patient swipe through their key metrics.
  • Persistent bottom navigation makes Home, Health, Action (record), My Sessions and Profile one tap away.
Screen 02 · Choose categories

Pick what to assess — then go

From a single screen the patient can choose any combination of Bradykinesia, Tremor, Facial Expression and Speech. Tasks across selected categories are merged into one continuous session, removing repetitive setup.

  • Each category card shows its colour code, icon and task count.
  • Plan usage is always visible ("3 / 3 sessions used") with a clear upgrade path.
  • Previous sessions live just below — filter by Start, Resume, Finished or Cancelled.
Screen 03 · Recording session

Guided, step-by-step recording

The recording screen brings everything the patient needs into one calm view: selected categories at the top, a progress and timer card, the full step list, and a live camera preview with the current task guidance.

  • Progress (steps completed and percentage) and a count-down timer are always visible.
  • Each category expands to show its individual steps — no guesswork about what comes next.
  • The camera preview shows real-time tracking with on-screen instructions like "Keep your right hand visible and tap your index finger."
Screen 04 · My Sessions

Every recording, neatly organised

The My Sessions screen turns every past assessment into a browsable, filterable history. Patients can resume in-progress sessions, revisit completed ones, or quickly find a specific date.

  • Each row shows the categories captured, the date, progress and current status (Not Started, In Progress, Finished, Cancelled).
  • "Videos saved" badges confirm that the underlying evidence is preserved.
  • The top counter (e.g. "63 sessions found") reflects the active filters in real time.
Screen 05 · Filters

Find exactly the session you need

The filters sheet slides up over the sessions list and lets the patient narrow results in two simple steps: choose categories, then choose a time range. Results update instantly.

  • Pick any combination of Bradykinesia, Tremor, Facial Expression and Speech.
  • Quick-pick ranges — Today, Last 7 days, Last 30 days — or a custom date range.
  • "Show 63 sessions" gives the patient confidence in the result before applying.
